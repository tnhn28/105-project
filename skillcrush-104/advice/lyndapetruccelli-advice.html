Hi my name is Lynda and I have some advice for you!

You're not supposed to know it all!

When you start programming, chances are you are doing something that you have never done before. It's a whole new world out there, and it's easy to get overwhelmed by how much you DON'T know. Don't let that stop you from starting.

Each and every new skill you learn will involve equal parts wonder and confusion. Not knowing why or how exactly things are happening is a sign that you are heading in the right direction. Frustrating as it may be, it's a signal that you have engaged in the problem solving process- it's time for you to embrace your inner Shaun T and Dig Deeper because clarity awaits.

Programming languages are just like any foreign language.

Did you take French or Spanish (or any other language) in high school? Remember all the vocabulary and grammar you learned? Instead of thinking of a programming language as hyper challenging tech, attack it the same way you would any other language- learn the vocabulary and how it all fits together.

Just like Spanish or French or Italian, the only way to truly learn is to immerse yourself in it.
