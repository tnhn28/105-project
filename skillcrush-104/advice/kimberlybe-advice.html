Advice for the Journey Ahead:

Through this journey, I am learning every day, not just about design & code; but invaluable life-lessons. So this is the advice I wanted to share with you…

  1. You’re not in competition with anyone else. So, don’t put unnecessary pressure on yourself. If you take longer to complete a lesson, so be it. It’s okay to go at your own pace. It’s better to take your time & actually understand what you've learned, instead of working to get through. You’ll need to truly comprehend everything in order to move forward, not just in your lessons; but in your career. Also, did you know you can utilize your technological skills & transfer them to basically any other field? Yup, it's true!
    "Whatever you’re passionate about, your technology skills are translatable & applicable.- - Kaya Thomas, Engineer" These skills are indispensable!
  2. When you can’t figure something out... You may grow frustrated, cry real tears, or think you’re not smart enough. STEP AWAY! Do something to take your mind off of it, when you revisit your work, you’ll have a renewed focus, a cleared mind & can channel the positive energy; you’ll find you’ll be able to solve your issue(s).
  3. Get out of your own way! If you find you cannot solve the issue(s) independently, ask for help! Guess what? There is absolutely no shame in reaching out to your Instructor(s) for help. You’re learning, so remember to be gentle with yourself. Besides, the Skillcrush Instructors are AWESOME! They give you the some serious gems/keys/tools to help you work through your problems. Moreover, Instructors have inside knowledge of what you’re experiencing because they’ve once been in the place you’re at now.
