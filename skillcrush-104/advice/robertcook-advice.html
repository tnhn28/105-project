Hi, my name is Robert Cook and I have some advice for you!

Have confidence. You can do this.

I think this is the cornerstone of any education. You have to have belief in yourself. Second, you have to have belief that you can overcome whatever programming challenge you face. Which leads me to . . .

The internet is your friend!

When you start learning to program, and even after you have learned the basics, problems will arise. Repeat after me: "The internet is your friend."

What do I mean? I mean, are you having a problem with your code? Try your favorite internet search engine. For example, when my floating objects were not behaving the way I had intended them to behave in my Holstee Manifesto project, I searched the internet for the answer (and I found it).

Practice, Practice, Practice

The headline says it all--keep practicing. Be consistent in your practice--practice regularly.
