Discipline is fun

"Fun" usually isn't a word associated with discipline, but they're really one and the same. Any time you set a goal (any goal!), a number of steps are required to get there. When the execution is aligned with what you envisioned, the whole process is enjoyable and satisfying. This alignment requires a certain level of what is commonly called discipline.

Of course unexpected things pop up from time to time, but when your vision and reality are consistently out of whack you can begin to feel not-so-great. Planning to work at the same time every day and never sticking to your schedule is one example - doesn't feel so great, huh?

Now, the caveat is when you set a goal for yourself that isn't actually in line with your true self. For example: pursuing a career that your parents expect from you, rather than one you're passionate about. In this case, discipline becomes a chore and suddenly turns into hard work.

All of this is especially true given that coding in general (and this course specifically) is fairly self-motivated learning. If you're feeling stuck, evaluate the reasons why you're pursuing this (hint: money alone probably won't be enough of a reason!) and find your "Why". Show the love and respect for yourself needed to achieve your dreams, and you'll find the process to be both fun and rewarding!
